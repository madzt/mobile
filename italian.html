Jake's College Kitchen Italian Recipes


Italian cuisine is generally characterized by its simplicity, with many dishes having only two to four main ingredients. It’s history dates back to the 4th century BC where each Roman citystate had its own approach to bread and pasta making. Italian cuisine offers an abundance of taste, and is one of the most influential cuisine on United States culinary. My favorite dishes I’ll be sharing today are:

  • Bow-tie pasta
  • Penne alle vodka
  • Bolognese pasta

Bow Tie Pasta

Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan. Then, cook bow-tie pasta in the water for 10 minutes. Warm butter and cream in the saucepan over low heat and let it simmer for 2 minutes. Next, whisk in garlic and seasonings for 1 minute. Lastly, add parmesean cheese and stir until cheese has melted and the sauce is smooth.


Ingredients


  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 teaspoons garlic minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 cups freshly grated parmesean
